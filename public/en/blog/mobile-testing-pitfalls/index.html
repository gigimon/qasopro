Mobile testing pitfalls

Why mobile scenarios differ from web and why emulators are not enough.

Back to all posts

Mobile apps live with unstable networks, varying screens, and limited resources. That context changes how we test.

The most common mistake is relying solely on emulators and ignoring real devices.

Key risks

  • Fragmented screens and densities.
  • Intermittent networks and slow connections.
  • Background states and recovery flows.

Practical advice

Keep at least a few real devices, test on real networks, and validate behavior when the app is interrupted.

Conclusion

Mobile testing is about user context, not just UI checks.
