Quality metrics that actually matter

Why bug counts mislead and which indicators tie quality to business risk.

Back to all posts

Counting bugs is a comfortable metric, but it is often toxic. It rewards finding defects for the sake of numbers and distorts reality for stakeholders.

The better approach is to track metrics that connect quality with delivery risk and speed.

What to track instead

  • Defect Escape Rate: how many defects reach production.
  • Lead Time for Changes: time from commit to production.
  • Regression overlap: recurring issues in the same module.

Context matters

A metric without context is noise. A higher DRE may be normal early on if the product is changing rapidly.

Conclusion

Pick two or three metrics that help decisions and are visible to the business, not just nice-looking charts.
