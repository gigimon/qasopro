Playwright vs Cypress: an honest 2024 review

Comparing architecture, speed, and maintenance so you can pick the right tool for your team.

Back to all posts

The test automation landscape is crowded, but Cypress and Playwright keep showing up in every serious E2E conversation. Both are strong, yet they solve different problems and impact team speed in different ways.

Short version: Cypress is great for a fast start and in-browser debugging, while Playwright wins on cross-browser coverage, parallelism, and multi-domain flows. Let’s break that down.

Architectural differences

Cypress runs inside the browser, which makes debugging pleasant and fast. The tradeoff is limited support for multiple tabs, domains, or complex auth flows.

Playwright drives the browser from the outside using DevTools protocol. That unlocks more scenarios and often gives you better stability and control.

What matters to your team

Ask yourself:

  • Do you need WebKit/Safari coverage?
  • Do you rely on parallel runs in CI?
  • Do you want Python/Java instead of JS/TS?

If the answer is yes to at least two, Playwright will likely save more time.

Conclusion

Both tools are solid, but Playwright scales better as products and teams grow. Cypress is a fine choice for a smaller scope, as long as you plan for future migration.
