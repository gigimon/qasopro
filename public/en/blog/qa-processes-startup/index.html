How to build QA processes in a startup from scratch

A minimal set of practices that delivers stable releases without heavy bureaucracy.

Back to all posts

Startups need speed, but speed does not have to mean chaos. The right QA foundation protects delivery velocity while preventing painful regressions.

The key is minimalism: keep only the practices that show impact within one or two sprints. Add more as the product grows.

Where to start

  1. Align on a Definition of Done and release quality criteria.
  2. Keep a short regression checklist for core scenarios.
  3. Track a few basic metrics: cycle time and critical defect count.

Minimal artifacts

You need living documents only: a map of critical flows and a risk list. Everything else can live in tasks or a lightweight wiki.

Common early mistakes

  • Introducing a heavy TMS too early.
  • Trying to automate everything right away.
  • Ignoring test data management.

Result: a lightweight process that keeps speed and grows with the team.
